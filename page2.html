HOW TO REPAIR IPHONE 5S

Repair Screen

Step 1. To get inside your iPhone 5, you need to use the tiny star-shaped driver and undo the small screws either side of the Lightning port. Be sure to keep them safe as they're easy to lose.

Step 2. Using the suction pad, lift the screen out of the case of the phone. this can be tough but if you use one hand to hold the case and the other to to pull the screen it usually comes out with a little effort. you can also use the plastic pics included to work the screen out once you've made a gap.

Step 3. Be careful not to lift the screen more than 90 degrees vertical as you may stretch the cables. Notice our replacement screen on the right and the fact there are three connector cables. These are hidden under the top metal plate inside our phone.

Step 4. The plate is held in place by three metal screws. Undo these with the standard cross-head screw driver and again, be very careful when you remove them as they're very small indeed.

5

Step 6. The plate latches on the left hand side so nudge it to the left then lift it out using one of the plastic pics. Don't force it out - it should come loose easily.

6

Step 7. With the plate removed you'll now be able to see the three cables we need to attach. As you can see on our replacement screen, they are layered so deal with the top one first.

7

Step 8. To detach the connectors, use one of the plastic pics or the flat blade screw driver and gently pry them off. This needs very little force and be sure to do it right from the edge of the connector.

Change Battery

1.The tool kit comes with a hex head screwdriver, which you use to remove two screws at the base of the phone



2. Here's the tricky bit - attach the suction cup to the bottom of the screen and pull to remove the screen from the casing. The home button end of the iPhone isn't attached inside - only the ear piece end is. This can prove quite tricky but I found that attaching the cup to the side of the home button and pulling hard while holding the outside of the casing did the trick.



3. Don't lift the lid more than 90 degrees as the other end is still attached as you can see below.



4. Inside you'll see a small metal plate held on by two screws. Use the included screwdriver to undo them.



5. Use a plastic pick to detach the battery cable connector.



6. The battery is held on with glue but a plastic sleeve underneath allows you to pull the battery out. You might need to use the plastic pic from the other side too.



7. Insert your new battery and connect it to the battery terminal.



8. Reinstall the metal clip and close the casing. Test the phone powers on then re-insert the base screws



9. Lithium Ion batteries should not just be thrown away
